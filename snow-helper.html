Triage Process Guide

The process for triaging ServiceNow tickets using the SNOW Helper.

1 Assignment ▾
  1. Review Pending Assignment board items
  2. Item need assignment?
    YesAssign ticket
    NoGo to Initial Action
Requires immediate self-assignment:
  • Tickets related to a Production issue/outage
  • Tickets submitted by a VIP
  • High priority or time sensitive tickets
  • Tickets that would not be picked up in time due to team members being out
Routine tickets (no immediate self-assignment needed):
  • Tickets that are not high priority or time sensitive
  • Tickets with an obvious team assignee (if that person is in the office)
2 Initial Action ▾
  1. Review Initial Action board items
  2. Items with initial action needed?
    YesComplete Initial Action
    NoGo to Status Update
Take appropriate action on all initial action needed items before proceeding.
3 Status Update ▾
  1. Review board items (top to bottom)
  2. All statuses current?
    YesGo to Quick Wins
    NoUpdate Status
Review ALL items top to bottom. Update immediately before proceeding.
  • Verify Short Description is still accurate/applicable
  • Verify most recent Work Note reflects current status and documents the most recent non-trivial activity
  • Verify item is in the correct Lane (primarily driven by incident state)
4 Quick Win ▾
  1. Review board items (top to bottom)
  2. Quick Wins exist?
    YesTake action
    NoGo to Prioritize
If a low effort action (15 minutes or less) can be taken to drive activity on an item, take it.

Note: order/priority does not matter. Taking low effort action on ANY item will help drive individual items to closure.
5 Prioritize ▾
  1. Review board items (by priority)
  2. High/highest priority?
    YesSpend 30 minutes dedicated on task
Switch gears to higher effort activity. Prioritize using your best judgement:
  • Items with a missed SLA should be prioritized
  • Items in danger of missing an SLA should be prioritized
  • Items that require assistance, feedback, or involvement from someone else should be prioritized
  • Most of your time should be spent on prioritized activities
